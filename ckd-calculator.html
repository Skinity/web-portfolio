Insight

We built a CKD risk calculator to support NHS clinicians and informed patients in managing chronic kidney disease.

The tool offers quick and clear eGFR results using standard formulas: CKD-EPI, MDRD, and both the Schwartz Paediatric Full and Bedside (Simplified) equations. It helps clinicians during consultations and enabled experienced patients to monitor their CKD status independently. Developed using an agile approach, the project focused on security, clinical accuracy and user-friendly design.

Functionality

MDRD Calculator

The MDRD Calculator (2005) estimates eGFR using serum creatinine, age and sex. the formula is: eGFR = 175 x (Scr)^-1.154 x (Age)^-0.203 x 0.742 (if female)

Since 2009 the National Kidney Foundation recommended the use of CKD-EPI over MDRD.

Functionality

CKD-EPI Calculator

Estimates glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) in adults based on serum creatinine, age, and sex using the CKD-EPI 2021 equation. It provides a more accurate assessment of kidney function than older forumlas, especially at higher GFR levels, and is widely used for diagnosing and staging chronic kindey disease (CKD).

Functionality

Schwartz "Bedside" (Simple) Calculator

Estimates eGFR (glomerular filtration rate) in children based on height and serum creatinine. It uses the simplified bedside Schwartz formula: eGFR (mL/min/1.73m^2) = 0.413 x (height in cm / serum creatinine in mg/dL)

Functionality

Schwartz Bedside Full Calculator

Estimates glomerula filtration rate (eGFR) in children using serum creatinine, height, and a constant based on the updated bedside Schwartz formula: eGFR (mL/min/1.73m^2) = 0.413 x (height in cm/ serum creatinine in mg/dL). This helps clinicians asses kidney function in paediatric patients quickly and accurately.
